This is a website created for the evaluation of the Computational Thinking module (CMT119) 2021/22. It consists of the following three pages:


  1. The first page is an introduction to the concept of Computational Thinking, drawn from the teaching of this module, and a brief reflection on my future career as a computational journalist. It is divided into four short paragraphs:

    • A brief historical contextualisation of the term
    • An attempt at a definition today
    • A practical example from our daily reality
    • A brief explanation of its relationship with computational journalism

  2. The second page contains the biography of one of the most important contemporary computer scientists, Guido van Rossum, the father of the Python programming language. The choice of this computer scientist is not random, but is dictated by the importance that this programming language plays within my degree course and future profession. Rather than following a biographical criterion, the webpage focuses more on the role played by its creation within the computer science community and the reasons for its success.


  3. The last webpage is a quick reflection on what this module teaches and how it will be applied in my future experience as a student. The reflection, although concise, tries to start from a comparison between computational and critical thinking, focusing on the differences and points of contact. Finally, it dwells on the benefits of starting one's experience as a 'programmer' with two languages such as HTML and CSS.
